Investment across Baltimore is uneven

Investment in Baltimore is uneven—fragmented by race, income, and geography. Some residents feel the effects of all three.

Capital flows are one indication of a community’s health and vitality. They also determine whether an area’s residents will have access to the services and resources they need.

This analysis maps and examines a broad range of investment types--federal, state, private and mission (i.e.capital from investors seeking both a social and financial return)—to show important differences in economic activity—and access to opportunity--between neighborhoods.
